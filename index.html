Fouad Street intersects with the Prophet Daniel Street, which is the same main intersection of the roads of the ancient city, which was established by the engineer “Dinocrates”, as he planned it according to the chess system common in the planning of Greek cities, and this type of planning depends on a group of orthogonal streets.


Bishop Achellous Tatius, author of the novel “Clitophon and Lucipe” described it in 400 AD, saying, “The first thing I noticed when I entered Alexandria from the Gate of the Sun (Rashid Gate) was the beauty of the city, and a row of columns connected its two ends. Advancing between these columns, I came in time to the square which bears the name of Alexander, and there I could see the other half of the city, which was equally beautiful. As soon as I saw the columns extending in front of me, other columns appeared forming sharp angles with the previous columns
